OWATE CONSULTING BUSINESS POLICY AND PROCEDURES

Definition of Career Counseling Relationship

Career Counseling is a partnership (defined as an alliance, not a legal business partnership) between the Career Counselor and the Client in a thought-provoking and creative process that inspires the Client to maximize personal and professional potential. It is designed to facilitate the creation/development of personal, professional, or business goals and to develop and carry out a strategy/plan for achieving those goals.

Terms of Career Counselor-Client Relationship

  1. Career Counselor agrees to maintain the ethics and standards of behaviour established by the International Coaching Federation (ICF). It is recommended that the Client review the ICF Code of Ethics and the applicable standards of behaviour.
  2. The Client is solely responsible for creating and implementing their own physical, mental, and emotional well-being, decisions, choices, actions, and results arising out of or resulting from the Counseling relationship and their Counseling calls and interactions with the Coach. While the Counselor is dedicated to delivering high-quality services, the Counselor cannot guarantee specific outcomes. The Client acknowledges that the Counselor is not liable for any actions or inactions, nor for any direct or indirect results of the services provided. This provision is intended to clarify the scope of the services offered and does not absolve the Counselor from their commitment to delivering professional and ethical assistance. Client understands Counseling is not therapy and, does not substitute for therapy if needed, and does not prevent, cure, or treat any mental disorder or medical disease.
  3. Client further acknowledges that they may terminate or discontinue the Career Counseling relationship at any time.
  4. The Client acknowledges that Career Counseling is a comprehensive process involving different areas of their life, including work, finances, health, relationships, education, and recreation. The Client agrees that deciding how to handle these issues, incorporating Career Counseling principles into those areas, and implementing choices is exclusively the Client's responsibility.
  5. Client acknowledges that Career Counseling does not involve the diagnosis or treatment of mental disorders as defined by the Canadian Psychiatric Association and that Career Counseling is not to be used as a substitute for counseling, psychotherapy, psychoanalysis, mental health care, substance abuse treatment, or other professional advice by legal, medical, or other qualified professionals and that it is the Client's exclusive responsibility to seek such independent professional guidance as needed. If the Client is currently under the care of a mental health professional, it is recommended that the Client promptly inform the mental health care provider of the nature and extent of the Career Counseling relationship agreed upon by the Client and the Counselor.
  6. Client understands that to enhance the Career Counseling relationship, the Client agrees to communicate honestly, be open to feedback and assistance and create the time and energy to participate fully in the program.

Cancellation and Refund Policy

Your appointment time is reserved just for you. If you must cancel or change an appointment, please let us know at least 24 hours in advance. Because we require this notice, the Career Counselor reserves the right to bill the Client the full session fee charge for a missed meeting and any cancellation or changes to your appointment with less than 24 hours notice. The Career Counselor will attempt in good faith to reschedule the missed meeting. Additionally, if you will be late, please inform us by email If you are late, the total fee will be payable, and the appointment will end at the scheduled time. Once a session has commenced, there are no refunds on Career Counseling and Consulting because once we spend our time with you and/or preparing for the sessions, we cannot get it back.

Scheduling Procedure

Career Counselor and Client will determine the time of the Career Counseling meetings and/or location based on a mutually agreed upon time booked on the website. The Client will initiate all scheduled meetings.

Confidentiality and Data Protection Policy

This Counseling relationship, as well as all information (documented or verbal) that the Client shares with the Counselor as part of this relationship, is bound by the principles of confidentiality outlined in the ICF Code of Ethics. The Counselor and Client acknowledge and agree that, in the course of the Counseling relationship, personal information may be shared. Both parties commit to treating all personal information with the utmost confidentiality and will not disclose or share such information with third parties without explicit consent. The Counselor will take reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality and security of the Client's personal data. Any records containing personal information will be securely stored and will be destroyed or deleted upon the conclusion of the Counseling relationship unless otherwise agreed upon. By entering into this coaching agreement, both parties acknowledge and consent to the processing and handling of personal data as described herein. The Counselor agrees not to disclose any information pertaining to the Client without the Client's written consent. The Counselor will not disclose the Client's name as a reference without the Client's consent. However, please be aware that the Counselor-Client relationship is not considered a legally confidential relationship (like the medical and legal professions). Thus, communications are not subject to the protection of any legally recognized privilege. Confidential information does not include information that:

  1. was in the Counselor's possession prior to its being furnished by the Client;
  2. is generally known to the public or in the Client's industry;
  3. is obtained by the Counselor from a third party, without breach of any obligation to the Client;
  4. is independently developed by the Counselor without use of or reference to the Client's confidential information;
  5. the Counselor is required by statute, lawfully issued subpoena, or by court order to disclose;
  6. is disclosed to the Counselor and as a result of such disclosure the Counselor reasonably believes there to be an imminent or likely risk of danger or harm to the Client or others;
  7. involves illegal activity.

The Client also acknowledges their continuing obligation to raise any confidentiality questions or concerns with the Counselor in a timely manner.
